## What is a Dewatering Pump?

A dewatering pump removes accumulated groundwater or surface water from an area where water presence creates operational, structural, or safety risks. Unlike sewage pumps designed for waste transport, dewatering pumps prioritise high flow rates and the ability to handle water containing sand, silt, and fine particulates without clogging or excessive wear.

Submersible dewatering pumps operate fully underwater — pushing water to the surface rather than pulling it — which eliminates priming requirements and allows deployment in deep excavations, flooded tunnels, and confined pits.

## Applications

**Construction site dewatering** — Keeps excavations, foundation pits, and trenches dry during groundwork, concreting, and laying operations. Essential during monsoon season and in areas with high water tables.

**Mining and quarrying** — Manages water inflow in open-pit and underground mining operations. Handles water mixed with fine mineral particulates without seal degradation.

**Municipal and infrastructure** — Flood control, storm drain management, tunnel dewatering, and emergency water removal from low-lying urban areas.

**Industrial process water** — Removes process water, cooling water, and wash-down water from factory floors, sumps, and retention pits.

**ETP / STP / CETP** — Transfers treated or partially treated effluent within water treatment plant circuits.

## Selecting the Right Dewatering Pump

The correct pump depends on three variables: the volume of water to be removed (flow rate in m³/hr), the height the water must be lifted (head in metres), and the nature of the water (clean, silty, or containing solids).

For construction site dewatering in standard conditions, a 2HP or 3HP submersible dewatering pump typically provides the right balance of flow rate and portability. For large-scale municipal or mining applications requiring sustained high-volume pumping, 7.5 HP to 15 HP models are available.
