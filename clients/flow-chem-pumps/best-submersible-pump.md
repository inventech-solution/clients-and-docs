## How to Choose the Right Submersible Pump

**Step 1 — Define the application**
Sewage and sludge with high solid content → sewage/sludge pump or cutter pump
Water removal from construction or flooding → dewatering or drainage pump
Abrasive slurry in mining or industrial settings → agitator slurry pump
Wastewater treatment plant circuits → wastewater pump or jet aerator

**Step 2 — Calculate flow rate and head**
Flow rate (m³/hr) = volume of liquid to be moved per hour at peak demand
Head (m) = vertical lift + friction losses in the discharge pipe
Always size to peak demand, not average — undersizing causes overheating and early failure

**Step 3 — Specify the liquid**
Clean or lightly silty water → cast iron pump
Corrosive, chemical, or saline water → SS304 or SS316
High solid content → check maximum permissible solid size in the datasheet
Fibrous or rag-laden waste → cutter pump mandatory

**Step 4 — Confirm power supply**
Single phase available only → single phase models (1–2 HP)
Three phase available → three phase models (all HP ratings) — preferred for sustained industrial operation
