# Dewatering Pumps for Construction

Flow Chem Pumps supplies submersible dewatering pumps specifically suited to construction site conditions — high water ingress, abrasive silty water, irregular operating schedules, and the need for portable, robust equipment that can be deployed quickly across a site.

Water accumulation on construction sites causes foundation instability, concrete pour delays, safety hazards, and schedule overruns. The right dewatering pump keeps excavations dry, maintains safe working conditions, and protects the structural integrity of work in progress.

## Why Construction Sites Need Dedicated Dewatering Pumps

Standard drainage or sewage pumps are not built for construction environments. Construction water typically contains sand, silt, cement fines, and fine gravel — abrasive materials that rapidly wear out standard impellers and seals not designed for particulate-laden water.

Flow Chem dewatering pumps for construction are built with:
- Hardened impellers resistant to abrasive wear from silty and sandy water
- Double mechanical seals protecting the motor from particulate ingress
- Robust cast iron or stainless steel housings withstanding site handling
- IP68 protection for continuous submersion in muddy, debris-laden water

## Common Construction Dewatering Applications

**Foundation and basement excavation** — Keeps pit floors dry during excavation and foundation laying. Essential in areas with high water tables or during monsoon season.

**Pile and caisson dewatering** — Removes water from bored pile casings and caisson excavations before concrete pouring, ensuring pour quality and structural integrity.

**Trench dewatering** — Maintains dry conditions in service trenches for drainage, electrical, water, and gas line installation.

**Tunnel construction** — Manages groundwater seepage in tunnel drives, particularly in coastal or alluvial zones with high hydraulic pressure.

**Road and infrastructure works** — Keeps road sub-base excavations and culvert installations dry during earthworks and concreting phases.

**Temporary works and formwork areas** — Removes accumulated rainwater and groundwater from formwork enclosures before concrete operations.

## Key Factors When Selecting a Construction Dewatering Pump

**Flow rate** — Calculate peak inflow rate based on soil permeability, catchment area, and expected rainfall. The pump must comfortably exceed peak inflow to prevent accumulation.

**Head** — The vertical distance from water surface to discharge point, plus friction losses in the discharge hose. Undershooting head specification causes the pump to run but not discharge.

**Solid handling** — Water from sandy or gravelly sites needs a pump rated for the maximum particle size present. Fine silts require attention to seal quality, not just impeller clearance.

**Portability** — On active construction sites, pumps are frequently moved. Lighter single-phase models are easier to redeploy; three-phase models offer better sustained performance for fixed dewatering points.
