# Cutter Pump

Flow Chem Pumps manufactures submersible cutter pumps for sewage and wastewater applications where solid waste, fibrous materials, and large particles would clog or damage a standard pump. The integrated cutting mechanism shreds incoming solids before they reach the impeller — preventing blockages and maintaining consistent flow in the most challenging waste streams.

## What is a Cutter Pump?

A cutter pump is a type of submersible sewage pump fitted with a rotating cutting system at the intake. As waste enters the pump, hardened cutting blades shred solid materials — rags, fibres, paper, food waste, and organic matter — into fine particles that pass through the impeller without causing damage or blockage.

This makes cutter pumps the preferred choice in applications where non-standard waste is regularly present: commercial buildings, hospitals, hotels, municipal sewage stations, and industrial effluent systems where conventional sewage pumps fail repeatedly.

## Applications

**Municipal sewage stations** — Handles raw sewage containing rags, wipes, and fibrous material that regularly block conventional sewage pumps. Significantly reduces maintenance callouts and pump downtime.

**Commercial and high-rise buildings** — Hotels, hospitals, office complexes, and apartment buildings produce waste streams with non-standard items that require cutting before pumping.

**Industrial effluent** — Food processing, textile, and paper manufacturing produce process wastewater with fibrous and solid content that demands a cutting mechanism to maintain flow.

**ETP / STP / CETP** — Used at intake stages in treatment plants to reduce solid particle size before primary treatment, improving plant efficiency and reducing filter clogging.

**Lift stations** — Where waste must be pumped over distance or elevation and consistent flow without blockage is critical to system operation.

## Cutter Pump vs Standard Sewage Pump

| Feature | Standard Sewage Pump | Cutter Pump |
|---|---|---|
| Handles rags and fibres | No — clogs | Yes — shreds before pumping |
| Maintenance frequency | High in fibrous waste | Low |
| Suitable for municipal sewage | Limited | Yes |
| Suitable for industrial effluent | Limited | Yes |
| Initial cost | Lower | Higher |
| Total cost of ownership | Higher (downtime + maintenance) | Lower |

For applications with predictably clean waste streams, a standard sewage pump is appropriate. Where fibrous or mixed solid waste is present, the cutter pump's lower maintenance cost and reduced downtime consistently deliver better total value.
